THE WARDEN'S SEAL — artifact notes

What it is: A forged customs clearance document — single folio, official Admiralty format, bearing what appears to be the counter-seal of the Port Warden's office. The document certifies the inspection and clearance of a specific cargo shipment that was in fact never inspected. It bears Orrus Vane's administrative identifier as the responsible officer, inserted there by whoever commissioned the forgery.

Origin: Fen Caldra had it made approximately twelve years ago, during a period when he was consolidating his position in Tiveck's gray market and wanted insurance against the people he was working with. He used a document forger who has since left Tiveck. The quality of the forgery is high — it would hold up to most scrutiny, though an expert examiner with access to the original counter-seal records could identify it.

Why Fen has it: He has never used it as leverage in any active sense. He has not threatened Orrus with it or made explicit demands. He simply, on one occasion years ago, made sure Orrus knew he had it. The knowledge has been sufficient. Orrus has been carefully cooperative with Fen's harbor operations ever since — nothing dramatic, just a consistent absence of complications.

Where it's kept: Fen does not keep it in the Salt Warren. He keeps it in a sealed envelope in a strongbox at a lodging house in the inland district of Tiveck, under a name that is not his. He pays the storage fee a year at a time. He has done this for twelve years.

Strategic value: The document ties Orrus to a specific shipment. The shipment, if investigated, connects to other things. Fen has never followed that chain all the way — he has enough of an idea to understand the danger and has chosen not to know more. Maren, with her Admiralty background, would likely recognize the significance of the specific cargo designation on the document if she ever saw it.

What a resolution might look like: The Seal and the Sunken Ledger are both leverage artifacts pointing at the same network. In combination they would be harder to dismiss than either alone. Whether that combination ever happens depends on what Maren and Fen eventually decide to tell each other.
