MAREN ASHVEIL — character notes

Background: Maren is in her late 30s. Worked for a decade as a licensed surveyor for the Admiralty before resigning under circumstances she doesn't discuss in detail. The official record says "voluntary withdrawal." People who were there say something on a mapping expedition went wrong and Maren came back alone. She hasn't filed an Admiralty survey since. She does freelance work now — private clients, academic commissions, the occasional job that pays well and asks few questions.

Current situation: She arrived in Tiveck three weeks ago, ostensibly to document the harbor's shifting sandbar patterns for a merchant consortium. The sandbar work is real. It is not the reason she came. She came because she found a partial reference in an old Admiralty archive to something called the Sunken Ledger — a manifest of controlled cargo movements through Tiveck's gray market over a thirty-year period. The Ledger would implicate people who are still alive and still powerful. She hasn't told anyone why she's actually here.

How she got to Tiveck: She hired Fen Caldra for passage on a small coastal vessel. She told him she was a surveyor. He didn't ask follow-up questions, which she appreciated. She has used him twice since for discreet errands around the harbor.

Relationship with Orrus Vane: She introduced herself to Orrus professionally, as a surveyor seeking access to historical harbor records. He was cooperative — almost too cooperative, she thought. He gave her access to thirty years of tide logs and berthing records without much friction. She has since started to wonder whether he wants her to find something, or whether he's watching her to find out what she knows.

What she's found so far: The berthing records contain a recurring vessel identifier — a registration mark that appears in cargo manifests but not in any official vessel registry. It appears sixteen times over twenty-two years. The last entry is dated seven years ago. She thinks the Sunken Ledger is a physical document, not just a metaphor, and she thinks it's somewhere in Tiveck. She thinks Fen Caldra knows more about the Salt Warren than he's admitted.

Personality: Quiet in groups, precise in conversation. Doesn't volunteer information. Takes notes constantly and keeps them in a cipher of her own invention that looks like surveyor's shorthand from a distance. She has a habit of arriving early to places and watching who comes in after her. She is not paranoid. She is experienced.

Physical details: Mid-height, angular face, ink-stained fingers. Carries a leather map case that rarely leaves her shoulder. Dresses in practical layers. The scar on her left forearm is from the expedition. She doesn't explain it.
