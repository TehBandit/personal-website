ORRIS VANE — character notes

Background: Orrus is in his early 60s. Has been harbor master of Tiveck for nineteen years. Before that, two decades in the Admiralty's port administration division. He is meticulous, institutional, and deeply invested in appearing legitimate. His record, reviewed by anyone who looks at the official documents, is clean. The official documents are incomplete.

The irregularity: Sixteen years ago, Orrus accepted payment — substantial payment — to look away from a series of cargo movements through Tiveck's harbor over several years. He did not ask what the cargo was. He adjusted berthing records, reassigned inspection schedules, quietly archived documentation that should have been forwarded to the Admiralty. He believed, at the time, that the arrangement was manageable and temporary. It ran for six years.

The Warden's Seal: Fen Caldra holds a forged document that connects Orrus to one of those cargo movements by name. Orrus has seen it. He does not know how Fen obtained it or whether copies exist. He has been quietly cooperative with Fen's operations for eleven years as a result. He considers this preferable to the alternative. He has not, in that time, done anything that would give Fen additional leverage, which he considers a form of partial redemption.

Relationship with Maren Ashveil: He gave her access to harbor records because refusing would have looked suspicious, and because he calculated that the records she'd see are incomplete enough that she won't find what she's actually looking for. He may have miscalculated. He has been watching her progress with careful attention. He is not certain whether she is working for the Admiralty, for a private client, or for herself.

What he actually wants: For the last sixteen years to remain exactly where they are. He is four years from the standard retirement age. He has a pension. He has a house with a garden on the south end of Tiveck. He would like, very much, to get there without anything unraveling.

Personality: Formal in manner, precise in speech. Gives the impression of thoroughness. Has a way of answering questions completely while not quite answering what was asked. He is not cruel and not ambitious. He made one decision under pressure and has been managing its consequences ever since.

Physical details: Tall, slightly stooped. White hair kept short. Reads without spectacles but holds documents at arm's length. Always carries a pocket notebook filled with his own administrative shorthand.
