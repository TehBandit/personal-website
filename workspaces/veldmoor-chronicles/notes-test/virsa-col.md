VIRSA COL — character notes

Background: Virsa is in her late 50s. She worked as a part-time records archivist at the Admiralty's records depot in Tiveck for eleven years before retiring. The depot's incomplete staffing — three days a week, never fully resourced — suited her preference for uninterrupted quiet. She knows the archive's gaps better than anyone, because she was the one who created them. Not through malice. Through omission.

The irregularity: Sixteen years ago, she accepted a small sum to misfile a series of incoming cargo inspection reports — not destroy them, just reclassify them under an obscure administrative category that no one ever searched. She told herself they'd be found eventually if anyone looked hard enough. No one looked hard enough. She has spent the intervening years not entirely comfortable with this fact.

Connection to Orrus Vane: Orrus was the one who arranged the misfiling. He didn't ask her to understand — just to do it, and to ensure the reference index was clean. She complied. She has seen him at civic functions since and they exchange civil nods. She suspects he has not forgotten what she knows about those records any more than she has.

What Maren Ashveil found: Maren located a cross-reference in the archive pointing to the misfiled category — a notation in the margin of a tide log, in handwriting Virsa does not recognize. Maren has not yet identified Virsa as the archivist responsible, but she is close. Virsa knows Maren is asking questions. She has considered approaching her first. She has not done so.

Current life: Retired. Lives alone in a tidy house three streets back from the eastern quay. Has a small vegetable garden. Reads extensively. Rarely goes to the waterfront anymore, though she can see the top of the harbor master's building from her upstairs window.

Personality: Quiet, careful, given to long pauses. Does not lie easily and therefore avoids questions she'd have to lie to answer. Has a strong sense of the difference between what is legal and what is right, and an uncomfortable awareness that she did not always choose correctly. She is not looking for absolution. She is not sure she deserves it.

Physical details: Small, gray-haired, deliberate in movement. Has a habit of straightening things — books, cups, the edges of papers — when she is thinking. Keeps meticulous handwritten notes of everything she reads, filed in an orderly cabinet she built herself.
