THE WRECKED COMPASS — location notes

What it is: A dockside tavern on the eastern quay, occupying the ground floor of a narrow stone building that predates most of its neighbors. Named for a compass rose painted on the exterior wall in a previous century, now so weathered that the directions are illegible. The interior is low-ceilinged, consistently warm, and smells of salt and woodsmoke. It has been continuously operated as a drinking establishment for longer than anyone can reliably document.

Who drinks here: Primarily dock workers from the eastern quay, small-cargo operators, and the kind of travelers who know to avoid the larger taverns near the western berths where Admiralty personnel drink. The regulars are loyal, unhurried, and disinclined to discuss each other's business in the presence of strangers. New faces are noted. Most pass without incident.

Fen Caldra: Drinks here several evenings a week. Has a usual corner table with a view of the door. The staff know him well enough that his cup is refilled without asking. He does not conduct business here in any formal sense, but several of his working relationships were first established over a table in this room. The Deepwater Guild, such as it is, meets here informally on the first and third Tuesday of the month.

Maren Ashveil: Has been here twice since arriving in Tiveck, both times to meet Fen. She finds it preferable to the harbor master's adjoining rooms, where Orrus Vane's staff occasionally have lunch. She has begun ordering the same thing each visit on the theory that being a regular, even a new one, draws less attention than being someone who studies the menu.

The back room: There is a small room past the kitchen that is available for private hire. It has a low table, four chairs, and no windows. The proprietor does not ask what the room is used for as long as it is left clean and the fee is paid in advance. Fen has used it three times in the past two years.

Atmosphere: Functional rather than comfortable. The kind of place where you can have a conversation without being overheard if you keep your voice down. The fire is always going in the cold months. The food is simple and reliable — salt-fish stew, bread, whatever the seasonal catch is. The ale is local.
