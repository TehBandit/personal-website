THE DEEPWATER GUILD — faction notes

What it is: Not a formal guild in any legal or institutional sense — there is no charter, no dues, no membership list. The Deepwater Guild is the name that has attached itself, over several decades, to the loose network of small-cargo operators, independent vessel owners, and harbor-adjacent traders who work the eastern quay. The name is used half-seriously, a bit of self-aware humor: they are the people the official harbor economy doesn't quite account for.

Who belongs: Approximately a dozen regulars, another two dozen people who associate loosely. Common thread: they all operate in the space between what is explicitly legal and what is explicitly prohibited, and they have developed, over time, a mutual-interest arrangement that amounts to information sharing and occasional coordination. Nobody is formally in charge. Fen Caldra is the closest thing to a central node, not because he asked to be but because he has been there longest and is trusted most broadly.

The Salt Warren: Several Guild members know the Warren exists, and that it serves as cold storage and transit space for gray-market cargo. None of them know its precise internal layout, or that Fen holds the only key. This is by design. Fen provides a service; the specifics of how he provides it are his own business.

Relationship with Orrus Vane: The Guild operates with a degree of tolerance from the harbor master's office that is not accidental. Orrus does not endorse their operations. He also does not pursue them aggressively, because disrupting them would create more visibility than ignoring them. This arrangement has never been stated explicitly by either side.

Relationship with Tiveck: The Guild is, in some ways, a reflection of Tiveck itself — a city where the formal economy and the informal one have coexisted long enough that the boundary between them is a matter of convention rather than law. Most Guild members have families who have been in Tiveck for generations. They are not transient. They are not criminal in any self-conception. They are pragmatic.

Current status: Quiet. The last six months have seen less unusual cargo movement than usual — Fen has been more selective about what he stores and transports, though he hasn't said why. Some members have noticed. None have pressed.
