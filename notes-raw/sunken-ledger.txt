THE SUNKEN LEDGER — artifact notes

What it is: A bound manifest — approximately ninety pages of heavy-stock paper, waterproofed with a wax treatment — documenting controlled cargo movements through Tiveck's harbor over a thirty-year period. "Controlled" in this context means cargo that should have been inspected, taxed, registered, and in some cases seized or refused entry under Admiralty regulations. The Ledger records that this cargo moved instead without interference, and names the individuals who facilitated that movement, the payments made to them, and in several cases the ultimate destination of the goods.

History: The Ledger was maintained by a man named Aldric Senn, who ran the primary gray-market cargo operation through Tiveck for three decades. Senn died eleven years ago of natural causes. His operation fragmented after his death. The Ledger was in a sealed crate in a Salt Warren storage chamber when the crate was abandoned by whoever inherited Senn's operation and subsequently couldn't keep it running. Fen Caldra found it while clearing the chamber six years ago.

What it contains: Among the entries are six years of payments to a harbor official whose description matches Orris Vane precisely, though Orris is not named directly — he is referred to by a position code that matches his Admiralty administrative designation. There are also entries for cargo that, based on the descriptions, matches controlled items whose disappearance was investigated by the Admiralty at the time and never resolved. The Ledger connects those investigations to Tiveck, which the Admiralty never managed to do.

Why it hasn't been used: It is extraordinarily dangerous to anyone who possesses it, presents it, or is known to be looking for it. The people whose activities it documents are, in several cases, still alive and still operating. Using it requires a plan for surviving having used it. Fen has not come up with one. Maren is closer to having one, though she doesn't yet know Fen has the Ledger.

Physical description: Navy blue boards, black cord binding, faded ink on the spine reading only a reference number. The paper is dense and slightly yellowed at the edges. The wax waterproofing has kept it in good condition despite years in the Salt Warren's damp.
